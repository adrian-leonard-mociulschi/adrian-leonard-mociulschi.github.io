Op-Ed Hub

Welcome to the Op-Ed Hub, the crown jewel of my writing collection! Now, don’t get me wrong—every piece I write has its own value, but this section stands out because it features editorials that have graced the front page of România Liberă, one of Europe’s most esteemed newspapers since 1877.

Each essay serves as a doorway to the present, offering a clear-eyed look at technology, culture, and the search for meaning in our algorithm-driven world. These aren’t just articles; they’re vibrant snapshots of thought, each one signed and published in a space that truly resonates with the public.

While the originals are in Romanian, you can also find English translations on ResearchGate and Academia.edu. I invite you to dive in—not just to read, but to really engage with these essays as rich spaces for contemporary insight.

Enjoy the journey!

From Consensus to Governance: Algorithmic Trust and Blockchain Delegates

România Liberă • Editorial — October 28, 2025

Power is no longer dug—it is signed. And sometimes, it’s signed by a dog. In the age of blockchain, authority is not imposed but negotiated through code and consensus. Delegated Proof of Stake replaces the miner with the validator, the vote with a cryptographic signature, and the leader with a protocol. From ancient numismatics to meme coins like Dogecoin, we witness a symbolic shift: governance becomes programmable, and irony becomes infrastructure. The digital agora is no longer ruled by charisma, but by credibility—and the ledger, once a tool of control, becomes a canvas of collective trust. In this world, even a joke can become a currency, and even a dog can wear the crown. Beneath the laughs, there’s a deep truth we can’t ignore: value isn’t tied to metal coins or paper bills anymore. Instead, it’s all about the networks of belief and the math that backs them up. What started as a fun experiment has evolved into something much bigger, challenging the foundations of power itself and making consensus the new ruler of the game.

Girl in a Jacket: A Story in Binary Code

România Liberă • Editorial — September 25, 2025

In this article I dive into an intriguing universe where I take a closer look at the semiotics of algorithmic mediation, all sparked by a seemingly minor detail: the phrase “Girl in a jacket” that struck me in a search engine snippet. What might seem like a simple mistake actually turns into a fascinating clue, shedding light on how our digital systems shape gender representations through things like metadata, ranking systems, and the design of user interfaces. I also challenge my readers to think about the ongoing presence of feminine stereotypes in the world of algorithmic design. By comparing the mysterious smile of the Mona Lisa with the calculated gaze of Sophia, I raise a thought-provoking question about presence, authorship, and the quest for symbolic equity in our AI-driven age.

The Economy of Vibrations and the Violin of Society

România Liberă • Editorial — July 15, 2025

Crises no longer knock politely at the door. They arrive together, overlapping like waves in a storm—AI reshaping work, climate change eroding certainties, geopolitics fraying supply chains. In this restless world, stability is no longer a straight line; it’s a fragile balance in motion, a vibration that never stops. Picture society as a violin. Four strings hold its voice: economy, technology, culture, education. Each has its own tone, yet harmony depends on their resonance. When one string tightens too much—through fear, speculation, or sudden shocks—the entire instrument trembles. The economy, for instance, is not a rigid machine but an oscillating system, sensitive to moods as much as to numbers. History whispers this truth: from Tulipomania to the 2008 crash, confidence has always been the invisible bow that sets the rhythm. And what keeps the music from breaking? Culture—not as ornament, but as a tuning key. It doesn’t silence crises, but it makes them intelligible, turning dissonance into meaning.
